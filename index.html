Introduction

I am a PhD student at the de Boer Lab currently working on the genomic regulation of cancer. Before starting grad school I worked as an undergraduate researcher in computational neurobiology at the University of Ottawa. When I'm not in the lab, you can find me on a soccer field, browsing the sci-fi/fantasy section at the library, or attempting to scale of the several mountains found near UBC.

Previous Work

A brief summary of some of my previous research experience. Click on the thumbnails for more detail.

Predicting Mutant p53 Binding

My current research involves using high-throughput molecular biology and machine learning to understand how mutations in the TP53 gene impact the development of cancer.

Designing a prototype for long term endotracheal intubation

As part of UBC's Engineers in Scrubs program I worked with clinicians to design and test a prototype endotracheal tube to prevent ischemic lesions after long term intubation, a problem exacerbated by the COVID-19 pandemic.

Automated Analysis of Neurobiological Images

I tested multiple approaches in order to design an automated pipeline for the analysis of confocal microscopy images.

In Silico Model of High Frequency Neuronal Discharge

My very first resesarch project involved using an in silico model of a pacemaker neuron, found in weakly electrical fish, to study the impact of voltage preturbation on the precision of discharge by a neuronal oscilator.
